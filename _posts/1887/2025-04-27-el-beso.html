üìù Extended synopsis with historical and cinematic context

This striking image sequence, created by Eadweard Muybridge in 1887 as part of his Animal Locomotion project, depicts two female models greeting and kissing each other. Published under the official title Two Models Shaking Hands and Kissing Each Other (Plate 444), it breaks away from traditional ways of representing the human body in the 19th century.

Beyond the affectionate gesture, the scene is extraordinary for its context: two women portrayed with respect, without eroticization, performing an intimate act before a scientific camera. Such a representation was virtually absent from a visual culture dominated by male and academic standards.

üé• Production context and historical value

Muybridge carried out this work in collaboration with the University of Pennsylvania, using a multi-camera system synchronized with electrical triggers. Models were specifically hired for these sessions, which documented hundreds of everyday or symbolic movements for scientific, artistic, and medical purposes.

Plate 444 stands out for its emotional and gestural focus, departing from more strictly anatomical studies. It has become the subject of gender studies, photographic history, and the visual representations of affection in 19th-century culture.
